# ⚛️ Franck–Hertz Virtual Experiment

A modern, interactive virtual laboratory simulation of the **Franck–Hertz Experiment** developed using **HTML, CSS, and JavaScript**. This project demonstrates the quantum nature of atoms by simulating electron collisions in argon gas and visualizing the characteristic current–voltage curve used to determine the excitation potential of argon.

---

## 📖 Overview

The Franck–Hertz experiment, performed by James Franck and Gustav Hertz in 1914, provided one of the earliest experimental confirmations of **Bohr's atomic model**. It demonstrated that atoms absorb energy only in discrete amounts, proving the existence of quantized energy levels.

This virtual laboratory recreates the experiment in an interactive web environment, allowing users to simulate observations, record experimental readings, and generate the characteristic Franck–Hertz graph.

---

## ✨ Features

* Interactive virtual laboratory interface
* Responsive and modern UI

## 🧪 Experiment Objective

* Study inelastic collisions between electrons and argon atoms.
* Observe the periodic variation of collector current with accelerating voltage.
* Determine the excitation potential of argon.
* Verify the existence of discrete atomic energy levels.

---

## 📈 Expected Result

The generated current–voltage characteristic exhibits periodic maxima and minima corresponding to inelastic electron collisions with argon atoms. Analysis of the graph yields an excitation potential of approximately **11.6 eV**, confirming the quantized energy levels predicted by Bohr's atomic theory.
